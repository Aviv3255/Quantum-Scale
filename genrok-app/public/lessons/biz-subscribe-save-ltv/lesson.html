3 More Ways to Increase Purchase Frequency

Subscriptions are powerful, but they're not the only tool in your arsenal

Strategy #1

Sell More Products (Cross-Sell)

After someone buys Product A, immediately offer Product B in a post-purchase flow. "Customers who bought this also loved..." Increase frequency by selling complementary items.

Example: Sell a yoga mat ‚Üí Offer yoga blocks in thank-you email
Strategy #2

New Product Launches (Launch to Customers First)

When you launch new products, email your customer list FIRST. They already trust you. They already bought once. They're 10x more likely to buy again than cold traffic.

Example: "VIP Early Access: New Summer Collection Drops Tomorrow"
Strategy #3

Loyalty Programs (Reward Repeat Purchases)

Give customers points, discounts, or rewards for coming back. Make them WANT to buy again. "Buy 5, get 1 free" or "Earn points on every purchase" keeps them engaged.

Example: Starbucks Rewards: Free drinks after X purchases

The Frequency Stack

Don't just pick one. Use ALL of these strategies together for maximum LTV:

  • ‚úì Subscribe & Save for core products
  • ‚úì Cross-sell complementary items after purchase
  • ‚úì Launch new products to existing customers first
  • ‚úì Loyalty program to incentivize repeat purchases

How to Implement Subscribe & Save

3 steps to turn your one-time buyers into recurring revenue

  • Step 1: Choose Your Subscription App

    Recharge, Skio, or Smartrr are the top Shopify subscription apps. Install one, connect to your store, and select which products to offer as subscriptions.

  • Step 2: Add a Discount Incentive

    Offer 10-15% off for subscribing. "Save 15% when you subscribe" converts hesitant buyers. The discount pays for itself with the increased LTV.

  • Step 3: Optimize the Subscription Experience

    Make it easy to skip, pause, or modify subscriptions. Flexibility reduces churn. Send reminder emails before charges. Keep customers happy and retained.

Pro Tips for Success

Default to subscription: Make "Subscribe & Save" the pre-selected option on product pages

Show the savings: "$120/year when you subscribe vs $160 one-time" makes the value obvious

Win-back campaigns: If someone cancels, send a discount to reactivate (e.g., "Come back for 20% off")
