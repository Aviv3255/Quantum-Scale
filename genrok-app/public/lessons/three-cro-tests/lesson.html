The Problem: Friction Hides in Plain Sight

Your product page looks fine. Your checkout works. But invisible friction is bleeding conversions.

Every scroll that hides the buy button. Every review section buried at the bottom. Every product that looks identical to competitors.

These micro-frictions compound. And the worst part? You never see them because they seem "normal."

1
The Floating Add-to-Cart
+$1.73M Revenue

When customers scroll down your product page to read descriptions, check reviews, or view more images, something terrible happens.

The buy button disappears.

They are interested. They are learning. They might be ready to buy. But the action is now hidden. They have to scroll back up. Find the button. Remember they wanted to buy.

That 2-second delay? That is where conversions die.

The fix is brutally simple: Make the "Add to Cart" button float. Always visible. Always one tap away.

2
Strategic Social Proof Placement
Instant Trust Signal

Most stores put reviews at the bottom of the page. By the time customers scroll there, they have either already decided or left.

The psychology is backwards. Social proof should appear BEFORE the decision point, not after.

Move your star rating and review count directly under your product title. This is the first thing customers see. Before price. Before features. Before anything else.

"4.8 stars from 2,847 reviews" creates instant credibility. It frames everything that follows.

Social proof works best when it shapes first impressions. When reviews appear immediately under the title, customers process all other information through that lens of credibility. At the bottom? Too late to influence their decision.
3
Novelty & Urgency Badges
+$1.1M Revenue

Your brain is wired to notice what is different. In a sea of identical product images, badges like "NEW" or "BESTSELLER" create instant pattern interrupts.

They signal: "This one is special. Pay attention here."

The Oodie tested simple badges on product images. Not flashy. Not overwhelming. Just small, tasteful indicators that certain products were new arrivals or top performers.

Badge Psychology at Work

NEW
Triggers curiosity and FOMO. "What did I miss? What is fresh?"
BESTSELLER
Social proof in two words. "If everyone wants it, maybe I should too."
LIMITED
Scarcity trigger. Creates urgency without being pushy or fake.
BACK IN STOCK
Implies previous demand. "It sold out? Must be good."
STAFF PICK
Insider recommendation. Creates trust through curation.
TRENDING
Real-time social proof. "Others are buying this right now."

Why These Three Work Together

Each test removes a different type of friction.

Floating CTA removes action friction (I want to buy, but the button is gone).

Social proof placement removes trust friction (Is this actually good?).

Badges remove attention friction (Which product should I look at?).

Combined, they create a frictionless path from landing to purchase.
